Behavioral journey maps — five user types
Each persona map uses the same structure: Stages, User thoughts & emotions, Barriers, Design interventions, Success metrics. These maps are action‑ready and testable.

Renters
Stages: Awareness → Explore readiness → Micro‑prep → Qualification → Decision
Thoughts & emotions: curious; anxious about credit; hopeful
Barriers: unclear eligibility; fear of rejection; documentation gaps
Interventions: simple pre‑qualifier quiz; rent‑to‑own pathway option; micro‑tasks with time estimates; friendly microcopy; credit‑improvement tips.   
Metrics: pre‑qualifier completion rate; document upload rate; conversion to pre‑approval.

First‑time Buyers
Stages: Discovery → Budgeting → Pre‑approval → Offer → Closing
Thoughts & emotions: excited; overwhelmed; risk‑averse
Barriers: cognitive overload; jargon; fear of hidden costs
Interventions: guided budget builder; “what this means” tooltips; one‑click cost breakdown; milestone celebrations; human advisor touchpoint before offer.   
Metrics: time to pre‑approval; drop‑off at KYC; NPS after onboarding.

Homeowners (refinance or second purchase)
Stages: Trigger → Compare options → Document refresh → Approval → Refinance close
Thoughts & emotions: pragmatic; skeptical about value; time‑sensitive
Barriers: perceived low benefit; friction in document refresh
Interventions: instant savings estimator; auto‑prefill from prior profile; “what changed since last time” insight; fast path for returning users.   
Metrics: estimator engagement; refinance application completion; time to close.

Affluent Borrowers
Stages: Discovery → Product match → Concierge onboarding → Approval → Portfolio management
Thoughts & emotions: efficiency‑driven; privacy‑sensitive; expects white‑glove service
Barriers: too many generic steps; lack of personalization
Interventions: tailored hero message; concierge contact; advanced product filters; compact deep‑dive analytics; privacy assurances.   
Metrics: concierge conversion; time to decision; retention for cross‑sell.

Investors
Stages: Opportunity scan → Deal qualification → Portfolio underwriting → Funding → Reporting
Thoughts & emotions: analytical; ROI‑focused; impatient with noise
Barriers: insufficient data granularity; slow underwriting
Interventions: investor dashboard variant with summary + deep dive; exportable deal packs; fast lane underwriting; alerts for portfolio events.   
Metrics: deal view to qualification time; underwriting throughput; investor retention.
