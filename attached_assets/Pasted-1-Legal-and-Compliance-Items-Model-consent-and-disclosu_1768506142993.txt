1. Legal and Compliance Items
Model consent and disclosure language for both soft and hard pulls; store the exact text version used.

Adverse action templates mapped to bureau reason codes and AUS outcomes; include delivery methods and timestamps.

State‑specific rules (Illinois IDFPR and any other states you’ll operate in) for fees, deposits, and disclosures.

Legal review of fee/refund policy, deposit terms, and marketing language by counsel experienced in mortgage/FCRA.

2. Vendor, Contract, and Data Flow Requirements
Vendor selection and contracts: tri‑merge vendor SLA, pricing tiers, rescore/supplement fees, sample report delivery, and support SLAs.

Test credentials and sample reports from each vendor to validate parsing and mapping in staging.

Idempotency and rate‑limit handling for bureau APIs; queueing and retry logic for transient failures.

Fallback plan (single bureau or manual process) if tri‑merge vendor is down.

3. Security, Data Storage, and Retention
PII storage plan: what stays in Replit runtime vs. managed encrypted storage (S3/Supabase).

Key management and secrets lifecycle using Replit secrets + cloud KMS.

Encryption at rest and in transit for all credit responses and consent records.

Retention policy and automated purge/archival to meet 3+ year regulatory requirements.

Data subject request process (access, deletion, portability) and consent revocation handling.

4. Auditability, Logging, and Evidence
Append‑only audit store with cryptographic hashes or signed entries for consent, soft_pull, commitment, hard_pull, and adverse_action.

Store raw bureau response IDs and full payloads (encrypted) plus parsed fields.

Exportable audit packages (CSV/PDF) for exams showing timeline, consent, and notices.

Automated reconciliation between pulls billed by vendor and pulls recorded in your system.

5. Operational Playbook and UX
Gating rules that define exactly when to require signed application, deposit, or purchase agreement.

Clear borrower messaging about soft vs hard pulls and any fees; visible in UI and emails.

Save & Resume and session persistence for multi‑step consent flows.

Support scripts and SLA for follow‑up when a hard pull fails or a borrower disputes a pull.

Training and SOPs for MLOs, processors, and compliance staff on the staged flow and adverse actions.

6. Monitoring, Testing, and Resilience
End‑to‑end tests in staging with vendor test accounts and sample reports.

Monitoring and alerts for failed pulls, high error rates, vendor SLA breaches, and unusual adverse action spikes.

Incident response and runbooks for data breaches, vendor outages, and compliance incidents.

Performance and cost monitoring to track tri‑merge spend per channel and per funded loan.
