
Friction Audit: Borrower Journey
LANDING PAGE (/)
1. Hero has two competing CTAs of equal visual weight

"Start Your Pre-Approval" and "Browse Properties" sit side by side. Users who don't know which to click may hesitate.
Fix: Make "Browse Properties" a text link or ghost button without the outline border, so there's one obvious primary action.
2. Six audience cards all look the same

Every card is the same size, color weight, and layout. A first-time buyer scanning quickly can't find themselves fast.
Fix: Visually highlight the 2 most common paths (First-Time Buyers, Current Homeowners) with a subtle border or "Most Popular" badge.
3. Bottom CTA duplicates the hero CTA

"Start Your Pre-Approval" appears twice. Users who scrolled to the bottom already saw it.
Fix: Shift the bottom CTA to a warmer, outcome-focused prompt like "See your options in 3 minutes" with "Start Your Pre-Approval" as the button.
PRE-APPROVAL FORM (/apply)
4. 14 steps feels long -- no section grouping

The step counter says "Step 1 of 14" which triggers a "this is going to take forever" reaction.
Fix: Group steps into 3 phases (Property, Income, Profile) and show "Phase 1 of 3" or remove the total count and just show "Step 1" with the time estimate.
5. Credit score question creates anxiety

"Roughly, what is your credit score?" -- many borrowers don't know theirs and feel judged picking a range.
Fix: Add a "Not sure" option that maps to a middle default. Add subtext: "Don't worry -- we'll verify this later. An estimate is fine."
6. "Below 640" label is stigmatizing

The lowest credit option says "Below 640" which feels like a label of inadequacy.
Fix: Relabel to "Under 640 -- that's okay" or simply "Under 640" with reassuring subtext like "Many programs are available at every credit level."
7. Monthly debts question lacks guidance on what to include

Subtext says "Include car loans, credit cards, student loans, etc. (not rent/mortgage)" but some borrowers also have child support, alimony, or medical payments.
Fix: Expand to "Car, student, personal loans, credit card minimums, child support. Don't include rent, utilities, or groceries."
8. "A couple more questions about you" is vague

The boolean pair step title doesn't tell the user why these questions matter.
Fix: Change to "These help us find programs you may qualify for" so the user understands the benefit.
9. Final step says "This will not affect your credit score" -- inconsistent with footer

The footer says "Soft credit check only - won't affect your score" but the final step says "This will not affect your credit score."
Fix: Standardize to "Soft credit check only - won't affect your score" everywhere.
10. Auth gate appears after the user has already answered everything

Being asked to sign in at the very end feels like a bait-and-switch. The user invested 3 minutes and now hits a wall.
Fix: Add a dismissible sign-in prompt earlier (around step 3-4) saying "Sign in now to save your progress automatically" -- so the auth gate at the end is expected or already handled.
11. Advisory panel is desktop-only

Mobile users (likely 50%+) never see the live DTI, estimated payment, or contextual advice.
Fix: Show a compact, collapsed advisory bar at the bottom of mobile that users can expand (like a "peek" drawer).
DASHBOARD (/dashboard)
12. "You're 10% ready to move forward" discourages new users

A brand-new user sees 10% and a nearly empty progress bar. Feels like they're already behind.
Fix: Either start at 0% with "Let's get started" framing, or hide the readiness bar until they've taken at least one meaningful action.
13. "View details" link goes to # (broken)

Line 862: <Link href="#">View details</Link> -- this link doesn't navigate anywhere.
Fix: Link to /onboarding or the pipeline page for the active application.
14. Dominant action card + journey checklist link creates split attention

The dominant CTA says "Get Started" but right below it says "View full journey checklist." New users may click the checklist link instead and get overwhelmed by 7+ steps.
Fix: Hide the journey checklist link for users who haven't started an application. They don't need to see the full roadmap yet.
ONBOARDING JOURNEY (/onboarding)
15. "Review Disclosures" and "Authorize Credit Check" both link to /e-consent

Two separate steps go to the same page. The user doesn't know the difference, and clicking one may complete both.
Fix: Either combine into one step ("Review & Authorize Disclosures") or give them distinct destinations.
16. All steps show "Get Started" buttons even when they're not actionable yet

Steps that depend on prior steps (e.g., "Upload Documents" before identity is verified) show an active button that might lead to a dead end.
Fix: Show "Locked" or "Complete the step above first" for steps whose prerequisites aren't met, instead of a clickable button.
17. Feedback form shown to brand-new users

The "Share Your Feedback" card appears even when a user has completed 0 steps. They have nothing to give feedback on yet.
Fix: Only show the feedback card after 2+ steps are completed.
